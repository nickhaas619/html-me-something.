How I Got Started

Growing up in any generation that had proffessional sports or popular animated cartoons, one would probably have a story about buying a pack of collectible cards.
I personally remember begging my parents for a $2 pack of baseball cards or pokemon cards everytime we went to the store.
Growing up a St. Louis Cardinals fan, I would go through my $2 pack of cards, and individually sleeve all the cardinals players I could find.
Before long, I ended up with hundreds of thousands of "worthless" cards that only value was sentimental...


... Or so I thought...




Fast forward to present time.
I stopped collecting cards in my teens. I then began collecting signed football helmets of my favorite players within the last 5 years.
That is when I noticed that certain sports cards were selling for 100's of dollars more than what I was buying autographed helmets for.
"How could that be?", "it's just a piece of cardboard, this is a real helmet" is what I thought to myself.
I dove deep and found out everything I could about the sports cards and collectible market. I realized there is a huge market for certain
players, rookie year cards, autographs, and game-used patches of jerseys, gloves and cleates.
Thats when I began my quest to getting back into the hobby I knew so well as a child, and also adding a very profitable aspect as well.
Now I only collect the cards of players I truly follow and look up to, and sell what I dont want to keep in my personal collection (PC).



Hobby Boxes?

There are so many different types of cards one can buy now. Almost every sport has a contract with some type of card producing company.
In the photo above you can see just a few types of boxes I have bought over the last few months. A box has a specific number of random cards for a sport.
Ones that have guranteed "high-end" cards are called hobby boxes, and ones that arent gurenteed but still possible are called retail boxes.
Pictured above is just a small portion of the hobby boxes i've purchased this year. A hobby box price can range anywhere from $200 to $2000 PER BOX!
Yes that is super expensive but sometimes the reward outweighs the risk of spending that much money. I have been lucky enough to pull cards out of boxes that i've later sold for $1000's! You can find hobby boxes at a local card shop (LCS). "Retail" boxes are what one would find at a store like Walmart, or Target, but good luck finding any.
There is a huge resale market for retail on ebay too! Even Pokemon, Garbage Pale Kids, and Magic The Gathering cards hold a huge value in todays online sale market.
If you still have some of your old cards you collected as a kid, go check them out! you'd be suprised what theyre worth today!



A Look At Prices

Sadly, the photo above is not cards I have sold, or been lucky enough to get, but notice the sales prices of some of these cards! These are sold "comps" for a rookie last year named Zion Williamson. He is one of the biggest up-and-comers in the NBA and his card prices are through the roof!
To check sold comps on your own:

  1. go to Ebay and have the card you want to look up with you.
  2. Type in the name of the player, company that made the card (ex. Topps, UpperDeck, Panini, etc.), serial number (if any), and some basic details such as: Auto, Rookie, etc.
  3. Hit that search button and set your "show only" tabs to sold items, and completed items
  4. Now you can scroll through listing that have already been sold and see what they have been selling for in the last few weeks/months!


There are many other high-prices players, new and old, and thousands of variations of cards that could worth big money! To alot of people, they see a worthless cardboard picture.
To me, I see gold!
